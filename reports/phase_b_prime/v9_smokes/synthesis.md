## The headline finding

The v9 hex-kernel + corner-mask architecture introduces a previously-undocumented **colony attractor** under self-play.  Two independent runs (S1, S2) found a step-500 trained model that crushed the v8full_warm anchor 86–91% in head-to-head eval — unprecedented in the v6/v7/v8 era, where no smoke ever produced a successful eval-gate promotion under v7full.  But those promoted models won **0.5–1 game out of 200** vs SealBot.  The eval gate's `wr_best ≥ 0.55` criterion measures relative improvement against the local anchor; under hex_kernel the local optimum is a colony-spam style that exploits a specific weakness in the bootstrap's distribution and has no general strength.

**Credit to user observation:** the "self-play games are generated by `best_model.pt` until promotion swaps the anchor" reframing is what surfaced this.  Before the SealBot survey we had attributed the late-run Class-4 regression to "self-play feedback drift," but the actual mechanism is a discrete promotion event at step 500 swapping the data-generating model from a strong bootstrap to a fragile colony-leaner.

Gate calibration is now itself a research target — see new "Class 5" below.** |

**Class 5 (newly identified) — eval-gate / colony-attractor coupling.** Under HexConv2d + corner_mask architecture, 500 self-play steps converge to a `wr_best ≈ 0.86–0.91` local optimum that wins via colony patterns (16-21% of wins on the eval distribution).  That gate criterion is satisfied, the model is promoted, and the new anchor is ~5pp WORSE on SealBot than the bootstrap.  Two interpretations:
* The gate's promotion criterion is too permissive for architectures with strong colony attractors.  Adding a `colony_wins_best / total_wins` ≤ X cap to the gate would block these.
* The HexConv2d architecture itself preferentially encodes colony patterns (the masking removes long-diagonal weights that contribute to threat extension; the residual hex 7-cell kernel learns local clustering features).  The architecture is the underlying problem, not the gate.

The smokes do not discriminate between the two.  Discriminating would require either (a) running a non-hex variant from v8full_warm anchor (v8full_warm anchor IS reachable from a Conv2d trunk; just override `model.use_hex_kernel: false`), or (b) adding the colony-fraction gate and re-running S1/S2.

## Recommendation for sustained Phase B' run

* **Revert hex_kernel + corner_mask:** they are NOT enabled in any sustained-run config.  v9 hex turn is fully falsified.  Master remains the right base.
* **Per-move rotation:** falsified as null on intrinsic policy.  Drop from sustained config; keep `rotation_cadence: per_game` (= §130 default).
* **Q2 jitter:** the only confirmed lever.  Sustained-run config = master + `selfplay.legal_move_radius_jitter: true`.
* **Buffer surgery (Class-3 priority 2 from §152):** subsample-on-push to cap `draw_target_fraction` at 0.5 — UNCHANGED, still pending.  v9 did not address it.
* **Eval-gate audit (new Class-5 follow-up):** before the next sustained run, audit promotion gate behaviour against a known-good v7full anchor.  Add `colony_fraction_max` parameter to `eval_pipeline.gating` to guard against future colony-attractor architectures.
